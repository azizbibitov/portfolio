Features

What it does

No forms, no dropdowns. You describe a transaction in plain language — by text or by voice — and Slate figures out the amount, category, wallet, and intent.

🗣️

Natural-Language Input

Type "spent 50 tmt taxi" or "+500$ upwork" and a Groq llama-3.1-8b model parses the intent, amount, category, and target wallet — context-aware of your real account names.

🎙️

Voice Entry

Dictate entries with SFSpeechRecognizer — English recognised on-device, Russian server-side — feeding straight into the same parser pipeline.

📴

Offline-First Queue

No connection? Entries queue locally in SwiftData and flush automatically when you're back online. A transaction's date is always when you entered it, never when it parsed.

👛

Multi-Currency Wallets

Run several wallets in different currencies with one active by default. Make cross-currency transfers at a stated rate, and switch wallets by voice: "use cash".

🔎

Smart Queries

Ask "show food expenses this month" or "expenses for [name] last week". Results render in an animated, in-place sheet that swaps content without ever dismissing.

☁️

iCloud Sync

SwiftData + CloudKit keep every wallet and transaction in sync across devices. Categories are auto-detected by AI, with lenient decoding so nothing ever crashes.

Technology

Tech Stack

Swift 6 with strict concurrency, an @Observable view model, and protocol-oriented seams that let the parser and storage layers be swapped without touching the UI.
